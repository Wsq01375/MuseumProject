Native American Art

Native Americans have a rich art history. Beginning with simple cave drawings to the present, the Native American culture has demonstrated artistry in painting, beadwork, jewelry, embroidery, and basketry. Upstate New York State was home to six American tribes known as the Iroquois Confederacy (Mohawk, Oneida, Onondaga, Cayuga, Seneca, and Tuscarora). Their remarkable legacy lives on in their artistry. Some rare historical and contemporary finds are housed on the museums below.

Native American Beadwork:

Beads were not originally native to American soil. The beads were important items of trade and later became incorporated into pouches, dresses, and decorative artifacts. The Native Americans are known for creating rich colors and intricate designs, typically depicting nature. More beaded artifacts can be found in Our Lady of Martyrs Shrine, Fonda, NY. It is situated on a 17th century Mohawk Indian village. The museum is a treasure trove of rare Indian artifacts, including beadwork. To plan a pilgrimage, go to https://www.ourladyofmartyrsshrine.org

Native American Contemporary Art

Contemporary Native Americans Artist are continuing their native legacy. Several notable Native American artists are: Noel Chrisjohn Benson (Oneida), Dawn Dark Mountain (Oneida Wisconsin), Jeremy Dennis (Shinnecock), Luanne Redeye (Seneca). The New York State Historical Museum in Albany, NY in continuing to add to its collection of contemporary Native American art, including painting, woodcarving and basketry. To plan a visit, go to http://www.nysm.nysed.gov
